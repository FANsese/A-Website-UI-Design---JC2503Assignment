Foreword

Interest

- this word seems to be none of my business for me in the past.

I used to be a boring person - I was not good at sport that many of my classmates playing, and I even not playing online games that really famous then. I just concentrate on study (and I didn't get a good grade nevertheless), my only 'hobby' is playing piano - for level examination. All of this made me unpopular at that time.

I remember my first real sense of interest is playing Rubik's cube. It was about ten years ago, I was watching a reality show called 'Super Brain'. I was really fascinated when I saw that tiny cube, brimming with vibrant colors, dancing on the players' hands and witnessed them racing against time to solve the cube in the competition. It was impossible not to be impressed by the speed and excitement they displayed, so I begged my parent to buy one then. Unexpectedly, I grasp some of the basic solutions quickly, and by playing and teaching others playing the cubes, I made a lots of friends and met many like-minded people, which made me more out-going. From this, I finally realized how interest mean to a person.

So far, though I play cubes less and this is not my main interest, I have many other interests now. But that colorful cube that once illuminated the lonely child always reminds me: the richness of life does not lie in chasing what others consider "hot", but in cherishing those moments that make your heart race - it can be the moment that witness the successful operation of the programs one has written day and night, it could be a moment that captures all the magnificent scenes in an instant, it could be the freedom that soaring in the sky... I finally realized: The essence of all of the interest, whether mediocre or not, is the gentle measurement of the possibilities of life.

Coding

Trying to make one's main job or main thing to study as an interest is not an easy task. When coding is mentioned, my memory always goes back to the time when I was learning in a robot club in junior high school. The language we learnt then is Arduino, and that was my first time I get in touch with code programming. Because I'm not good at abstract thinking, writing code has always been a painful experience for me. For that, at that time, I thought to myself, I would never, under any circumstances, engage in any work related to writing code in the future.

But later, the outcome was just like it is now. Due to various reasons, I was assigned to the Software Engineering major. For a student like me who has almost no foundation on coding, catching up courses is usually not easy, especially in the first year of university. This case was altered a bit in the first summer holiday of university. I was taken to my uncle's home for some further study, and I learned some really different things there, like linear prediction and training some prediction model. The tasks he gave were really hard for me, but after I tried my best and finally solved them, a sense of achievement always arises, and I finally discovered the interest of programming.

For now, coding really becomes one of my interests, especially writing html codes - it's really a very fulfilling thing that under one's own editing, the content of the page becomes increasingly rich and the style becomes increasingly beautiful. The coordinate system that once got lost in the error messages of Arduino was finally re-anchored by CSS selectors. When the gradient color gently blends within the arc of border-radius, one realizes that those "!important" that make the page tremble are merely the predestined fates that fate has already set.

Photographing

Though from the time when I was in primary school I start to frequently shoot some landscape and other thing, I suppose it was right after I finished the high school entrance examination that I began to take photography as a hobby. At that time, I spent almost all of my pocket money - I spent nearly six years saving it, to buy a GoPro8. That was my first photography equipment in the real sense. In the following five years, till now, I've buy a lot of different photography equipments, and also sold many of them, but since this gopro means too much for me, though it has almost no shooting ability anymore due to the aging of the components, I still keep it.

And here's a group photo of all my photography devices!

Sometimes when I looked at them, I can't help but sigh - these devices have accompanied me to so many places, witnessed and recorded so many gorgeous sceneries. In their memory cards, there are the azure blue of the countless lakes in Jiuzhaigou, the endless emerald green when crossing the mountains and ridges, the dark grey of the reinforced concrete structures in the city center, the warm yellow that covers the sky in the morning and evening... There is the dance of the bird wings captured in 8x slow-motion during the moment when the migratory birds fly; and there is also the alternation of day and night condensed into 5 minutes during my high school renovation project, which lasted for a month... The metal coating on the shutter button has turned into the shape of a river changing its course, while the lens coating is revealing the latitude and longitude patterns of the Gobi salt lake crystals. The memory card counter is still recording the geological sedimentation rate for all the dusk that has not arrived.

Except simply shooting photos, I also shoot videos somethmes. Occasionally when I'm in the mood, I may choose a topic, get out to shoot some video, edit them together, and post on some social platforms like Moment or Video Channel on Wechat, on Bilibili and so on. Though few people watch them, I still make them, to enjoy the process of making a video.

Nowadays, I often place my equipment on the table, treating it as a piece of decoration. These things are now quietly lying on the desk charging, like a group of soldiers who have just taken off their armor - the UV filter of the Canon lens still retains the coldness of the mist on Mount Emei, the red button of Action4 has been soaked with the salty and dampness of Palawan Beach in Singapor, and each scratch on the carbon fiber blades of the drone is still remembered as the wind speed when flying with the flock of migratory birds over Jiuzhaigou. Sometimes, when cutting clips late at night, I suddenly notice that the so-called love is just sharing the same set of vital signs with these metal objects: They remember all the shapes of light for me, and I am responsible for finding a coordinate system for those lost pixels to return home.

Flying Drones

During the summer holiday before I began my high school life, I get my first drone - a Mavic air 2. Getting it is truly a dream-level thing for me since before that I really want a drone so bad, eagering to see what the world looks like in the sky. I remember the first time I got it in my hands, I carefully opened the package, read the instructions carefully, and couldn't wait to go to an open area near my home to conduct a test flight. My mood at that time could be described as excited, because it meant that I could observe the world from a new perspective.

When the intelligent hovering function of the drone could no longer accommodate the ambition of soaring through the clouds, I began to explore the concept of FPV drones - I was truly fascinated by that sense of freedom of soaring freely in the sky. After I accidentally encountered a very cheap FPV drone package half year ago, flying a FPV drone became my latest interest.

Unlike normal aerial drone, the threshold for getting started with fpv drone is high. The full manual operation determined the difficulty of controlling it, so for most people, to control it properly and to do some stunt flight action, practicing the operation on the simulator for at least one month is a must. Since it may easily broke down when crashed under high speed of flying, mastering basic welding, assembly and parameter adjustment skills is also necessary. By flying fpv drones, I not only enjoyed the happiness to fly freely and sense accomplishment for successfully doing some flight action, but also I grasp some relevant hardware knowledge.

While the intelligent hovering algorithm of the Mavic Air 2 was still meticulously weaving a perfect geometric map in the cloud, my neurons had already begun to deconstruct the grammar of gravity - those spectral fragments torn apart by the snowflakes of the FPV goggles, those motor parameters tempered at the melting point of solder, eventually teaching me: True flight is never the solo dance of the drone, but rather a fugue composed jointly by the carbon fiber framework and the dopamine neural synapses. Each circuit board re-welded after a crash is a philosophical argument submitted by humanity to the three-dimensional space; Each dizzying experience of crossing the clouds is a comma trembling in the decoding of Einstein's field equations by the physical body.

Music

Though from the time when I was five I began to play piano, and most of the time then I mainly dealing with classical music, whether it is playing or appreciation, but at that time I seldom listening music for relaxation or for interest. Until when I was eleven, I was encountered a rhythm game called Rolling Sky. The background music was really fascinate me then, and I think that's precisely how I began to really fond of music, especially electric music. When it comes to my favorite type of music, simply saying "electric music" might be too general. For me, Future Bass and Melodic Dubstep are the music styles that I like the most.

Future Bass

While the twelve-tone equal temperament of classical music still adheres to the contrapuntal rules on the piano keys, Future Bass has used the acoustic chaos of sawtooth waves and square waves to open up the quantum world of sound for me. This music genre that emerged in 2006 uses a resonant frequency of 120-145 BPM to transform human voices into digital-era lyric poems. Its sound design is revolutionary - through LFO control of the filter to create a "flashing sensation", it is like putting nebulae into the matrix of a synthesizer. Canadian producer Far Out's "Constellation" is a perfect example of this aesthetic: he injects the anime-like cuteness of Kawaii Bass into the framework of house music, like using neon beams to draw a Möbius loop on the sound spectrum, creating a sonic landscape where sweetness and chaos coexist.

Melodic Dubstep

When one desires a more intense mental tremor, Melodic Dubstep (Melodic Echo Bass) becomes the antidote to my soul. As a branch of Dubstep, it retains the intense Wobble Bass of Brostep, but grows the softness of a harpsichord within the sonic cracks. The American musician ILLENIUM's "Fractures" perfectly embodies this contradictory aesthetic: When the violent fragmented beats pound the chest cavity, the soothing melody emerges from the cracks in the sound wall, like transparent roses blooming in the ruins of a nuclear explosion. This "existence of both brutality and healing" trait is like weaving a lullaby with steel bars - when the 430Hz screeching bass and the female vocal chanting resonate at opposite ends of the spectrum, the Cartesian coordinate system of the physical world completely collapses, and the auditory experience transforms into a function of the ratio of dopamine and melatonin.

From Chopin's nocturnes to the vocoder of Future Bass, from Beethoven's "Symphony of Fate" to the pulsating bass of Dubstep - my musical journey is essentially a revolution in sensory topology. When Far Out uses side-chain compression to create rhythm pulses like those of a space capsule docking, and when ILLENIUM plants transparent flower buds in the sonic ruins, they are all proving that the essence of all music is the gentle mapping of infinite possibilities by humans using limited technology.
